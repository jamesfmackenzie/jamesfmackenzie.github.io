The GameCube's video output story is more complicated than it first appears. The best setup depends heavily on which hardware revision you own and whether you care about 480p, HDMI, RGB, or CRT-friendly analogue output.

## Two Main Hardware Revisions

The first distinction to make is simple:

- `DOL-001`
- `DOL-101`

The important difference is that **DOL-001** includes the **digital out** port. If your console has that port, it unlocks the best output options and you should strongly consider using it.

## Resolution Notes

GameCube output is often discussed as if it were just 480i, but that is incomplete.

Key point:

- many games, especially NTSC titles, support **480p**
- if a game supports 480p, that is usually the mode you want
- your video chain needs to handle both 480i and 480p sensibly

## Best Options for Consoles with Digital Out

For a digital-to-digital setup, a **GCVideo** solution such as a **Carby** or **Prism** is the most obvious modern choice.

That section of the final article should explain:

- why GCVideo is attractive
- how it handles 480i and 480p
- what the deinterlacing options are
- why it is more convenient than rare original digital accessories

If HDMI is not what you want, a solution like the **MK-II** can still make sense because it exposes Wii-style analogue output options.

## Other Cable Options

There is also room to discuss:

- official and unofficial component cables
- D-terminal solutions
- SCART options
- VGA-oriented solutions

One practical point worth keeping:

- some official-style digital video solutions do not carry audio
- audio may still need to come from the analogue AV port

## Best Options for Consoles Without Digital Out

If your GameCube lacks digital out, you are limited to the analogue AV port.

That leads to a major regional difference:

- **PAL GameCubes** can output RGB over analogue and pair well with the official SCART cable
- **NTSC GameCubes** do not offer the same RGB path, which leaves them with much weaker analogue options

That is a strong argument for treating PAL and NTSC buying advice separately.
