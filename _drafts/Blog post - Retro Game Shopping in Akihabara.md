Games Stores
 
- Super Potato - great selection, tourist pricing. Multi floors, but all are small. Has some “holy grail” things e.g. NES and SNES unit to switch games. Also has an arcade upstairs? Show a photo of how much the N64 was marked up
- Book off - large store, that just happens to have many games - and some retro games - on the ground floor. Maybe a better price vs Super Potato? Has *LOTS* of used games PS2/PS3/Vita/Wii on the ground floor, alongside modern games - just tons. But N64 very small and SNES a bit small. Hardware really nothing apart from a few handhelds. On the plus side, lots of anime/manga etc on the upper floors
- Retro game camp - Small store with two floors. Prices were cheaper than super potato but more expensive that Surugaya, beep etc. Well ordered and well taken care of. Had a lot of selection of “mainstream” consoles - lots of N64, Famicon, PlayStation, Saturn etc bagged and ready to go. Compare Saturn price or N64 price to Super Potato, and also BEEP/Surugaya
- BEEP - Small store in a basement. GREAT for arcade and hardware. Small but lots of interesting hardware - I was able to pick up an N64 for a good price (show the photo and/or talk about the price). Had some total “holy grail” PC hardware - include MSX, PC88, PC98, X68000
- Surugaya - I tried Surugaya. Tiny downstairs section on video games. Had some really nice speciality items and mint boxed copies of rare or limited edition games. Price is also great. But store is small and didn’t have a huge amount of (for example) mainstream retro consoles or hardware 

Shinjuku

- Surugaya - bigger store on the 5th floor of a shopping mall. Video games section also bigger vs the akihabara store. Prices good, but perhaps marginally more than the akihabara store. But more selection. That said, I did pick up some good bargains here -  

Conclusions

- Loads of amazing selection. Awesome to just browse 
- Prices are good but vary wildly - watch out for tourist pricing
