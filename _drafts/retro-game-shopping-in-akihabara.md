Akihabara is one of the most enjoyable places in the world to browse retro games, but the stores are not interchangeable. Some are great for mainstream consoles, some are better for obscure hardware, and some are simply more expensive than tourists expect.

## The Basic Pattern

Akihabara offers:

- huge variety
- lots of fun browsing
- wildly inconsistent pricing

That means the best strategy is to know which shops are good for which categories rather than assume they are all equivalent.

## Store Notes

### Super Potato

- iconic shop with lots of retro atmosphere
- strong selection
- often expensive, especially for tourists
- useful for browsing and photos, not always for bargains

### Book Off

- much broader used media store that happens to have games
- excellent for sheer volume of PS2, PS3, Vita, and Wii software
- not always the best destination for niche retro hardware

### Retro Game Camp

- tidy, well-presented stock
- solid mainstream retro console selection
- prices better than Super Potato, but not necessarily the cheapest overall

### BEEP

- one of the most interesting shops for hardware
- strong for arcade and Japanese computer gear
- good place to spot unusual machines and genuinely exciting finds

### Surugaya

- can be excellent value
- some branches are tiny, others much better
- often good for boxed or more unusual items

## Broader Takeaway

Akihabara is still worth visiting even when prices are uneven, because the density of interesting stock is hard to match anywhere else.
