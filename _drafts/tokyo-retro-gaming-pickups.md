I already posted a thread about these pickups, but there is enough here for a proper blog post too. Despite some obvious tourist pricing in Tokyo, there were still some great finds.

## Highlights

### Nintendo 64 Bundle

At BEEP in Akihabara I found:

- an unboxed Nintendo 64 with controller for **JPY 6,950**
- a second matching controller for **JPY 680**

It needed a light clean-up, but it was in very solid shape.

### Wave Race

At Retro Game Camp in Akihabara, I found a copy of **Wave Race** for **JPY 800** in great condition.

### Sega Saturn

At Surugaya in Shinjuku, I picked up a boxed Sega Saturn for **JPY 14,800**, reduced to **JPY 13,455** after the tax rebate.

I also found some Saturn software surprisingly cheaply, including **Sega Rally** for **JPY 400**.

### PocketStation

One of the more unusual finds was a **Sony PocketStation** from a bargain bin for **JPY 500**. It was missing the flip cover, but at that price it was hard to complain.

### PS2 DVD Remote

Another tiny but satisfying pickup was a **PS2 DVD remote and IR receiver** for **JPY 200**.
