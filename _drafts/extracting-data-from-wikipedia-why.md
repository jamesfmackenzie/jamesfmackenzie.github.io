Wikipedia is one of the largest freely accessible collections of human knowledge ever assembled. But reading articles is only one use case.

## Why Extract Data at All?

Sometimes the interesting part is not the article itself, but the ability to:

- analyse a category of things
- aggregate entries into a dataset
- pivot or filter information across many pages
- combine Wikipedia with other data sources

## What This Intro Post Should Do

This draft works best as the opening piece for a small series. It should explain:

- why Wikipedia is attractive as a data source
- what kinds of extraction are possible
- the trade-offs between API queries, Wikidata, DBpedia, and scraping
