PVMs and BVMs get most of the attention in retro gaming circles, but they are not the only way to get a great picture. A good multisync CRT can be cheaper, more flexible, and in some setups more practical.

## The Basic Argument

Multisync monitors deserve more attention because they can offer:

- native support for 15kHz RGB
- support for multiple scan rates and resolutions
- sharp image quality
- strong scanline presentation without extra processing

That makes them a compelling alternative to the endless PVM and BVM hype.

## Example Setup

The note here is based on a **Microvitec** multisync CRT monitor.

With a PlayStation 2, the signal chain is:

1. PS2 via RGB SCART
2. Sync Strike to clean up the sync and break out VGA-style output
3. 15kHz multisync monitor

Because the monitor already supports 15kHz RGB, there is no need for line doubling or deinterlacing.

## Why This Matters

A setup like this can deliver an excellent picture without the cost or complexity of more fashionable broadcast monitors.
