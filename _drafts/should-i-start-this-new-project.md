One of the easiest ways to get overwhelmed is to keep starting new projects without being clear on what success would even look like.

This draft is built around a simple question from the Getting Things Done style of planning:

## What Does Wild Success Look Like?

Before starting a project, it is worth being specific about the outcome:

- What would make this clearly worth doing?
- What would the finished version actually look like?
- What would success look like in a month or a year?

If the answer is vague, the project is probably still too fuzzy.

## Why This Matters

New projects are attractive because they feel full of possibility. But that same ambiguity is what makes them dangerous. Without a clear picture of success, it is easy to sink time into something that never becomes useful or enjoyable.
