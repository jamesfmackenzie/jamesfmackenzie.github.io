The MiSTer FPGA USB Hub is one of the least glamorous MiSTer add-ons, but also one of the most useful. A bare DE10-Nano board is enough to get MiSTer running, but it becomes much easier to live with once you can connect multiple controllers, keyboards, storage devices, and other peripherals without improvising a mess of adapters.

That is exactly what the USB Hub solves.

### Why the USB Hub matters

The USB Hub is important because it turns MiSTer from a technically functional board into a much more practical everyday system.

It gives you room for:

- multiple controllers
- keyboard and mouse input
- storage devices
- Wi-Fi or Bluetooth adapters
- other USB accessories that quickly become part of a real MiSTer setup

That makes it one of the most sensible first upgrades for anyone moving beyond basic testing.

### Where it fits best

The USB Hub matters most when:

- you want a living-room-friendly MiSTer
- you switch between multiple systems and controller types
- you want the hardware to behave more like a finished console or computer and less like a development board

In practice, that describes a lot of real MiSTer builds.

### Why I rate it highly

Some add-ons are specialized. The USB Hub is different because it improves the basic usability of almost every MiSTer setup.

It is not the most exciting board, but it is one of the most sensible.
